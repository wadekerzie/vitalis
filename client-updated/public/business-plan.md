## Executive Summary

Vitalis is a subscription-first longevity and biometric optimization clinic designed for the high-net-worth market in Dallas, Texas, with a franchise platform architecture built for national expansion. The flagship location targets the Park Cities / Preston Hollow corridor — home to over 72,400 millionaires and a median household income exceeding $156,000 — where concierge medicine already commands $24,000 per year per patient.

The business operates on two parallel value creation paths that are presented together, not sequentially, because both are fundamental to the investment thesis.

**Path One — Flagship Unit Economics:** A single 2,500 square foot location generates $2.1 million in annual revenue at a 38% EBITDA margin once mature (Month 18), with break-even achieved at Month 8. The model is direct-pay only, eliminating insurance billing complexity and protecting margins. Dr. Truei, an established concierge medicine physician, serves as Medical Director, providing immediate clinical credibility and patient referral potential.

**Path Two — Franchise Platform:** After proving the model across 2–3 corporate-owned locations, Vitalis licenses its proprietary operating system — including the AI-powered Vitalis Score engine, standardized clinical protocols, and HIPAA-compliant technology infrastructure — to franchise operators. By Year 5, a 15-location system (3 corporate, 12 franchise) generates $3.4 million in franchisor EBITDA and supports an enterprise valuation of $34–41 million at standard healthcare franchise multiples of 10–12x EBITDA.

The total capital requirement to open the Dallas flagship is **$865,400**, which includes build-out, equipment, working capital, and pre-opening costs. The AI architecture engagement (scoped separately) is the foundational investment that makes the franchise platform possible — it is not overhead, it is the moat.

## The Market Opportunity

### Dallas: The Right City

Dallas is not merely a convenient starting point. It is the optimal launch market for a premium longevity platform. The city ranks among the top five metropolitan areas in the United States for millionaire concentration, with 72,400 millionaires and 135 centi-millionaires (individuals with $100M+ in assets) as of 2024. The Park Cities submarket — Highland Park and University Park — represents one of the highest concentrations of household wealth in the American South, with median household incomes of $156,000 and a median age of 41, placing the core demographic squarely in the 40–65 age range that drives longevity spending.

Concierge medicine is already normalized in this market. MD2, operating in Park Cities, charges $24,000 per year per individual for primary care access alone. The Vitalis membership model — which delivers measurable, data-driven optimization outcomes rather than reactive sick care — is positioned as the logical next layer of a high-net-worth individual's health stack.

### The Longevity Market

The global longevity clinic market is projected to grow from $5.35 billion in 2025 to $9.55 billion by 2030, representing a 12.2% compound annual growth rate. The adjacent medical spa market is larger still, projected to reach $59.4 billion by 2033 at a 13.2% CAGR. Premium players in this space — Next Health, Fountain Life, Cenegenics, Human Longevity Inc. — have demonstrated that affluent consumers will pay $10,000–$100,000 per year for personalized longevity protocols when the value proposition is clear and the experience is exceptional.

What none of these players have built is a franchise-ready, AI-native operating system that allows the model to replicate with fidelity. That is the Vitalis differentiation.

## The Vitalis Model

### Services

Vitalis operates as a direct-pay, membership-first clinic offering the following service categories, all confirmed for the flagship:

- **IV Therapy & Infusion Protocols** — customized nutrient, hydration, and recovery infusions
- **Hormone Optimization** — comprehensive panels, peptide protocols, testosterone and estrogen management
- **Longevity Biomarker Panels** — advanced lab work including inflammatory markers, metabolic panels, micronutrient analysis, and telomere assessment
- **Body Composition & Performance** — DEXA scanning, InBody analysis, visceral fat tracking
- **Recovery Modalities** — cold plunge, red light therapy, cryotherapy
- **Surgical Recovery Programs** — structured post-operative optimization packages (a natural referral from surgical networks)
- **Executive Physicals** — comprehensive half-day assessments for C-suite clients

### The Vitalis Score

Every member receives a proprietary Vitalis Score — a 0–100 composite vitality index calculated from five biomarker domains: Metabolic, Inflammatory, Nutrient Status, Hormonal Balance, and Recovery Capacity. The score updates with each visit and lab draw, creating a longitudinal optimization narrative that is unique to each member. This score is the product. It is what members pay to improve, track, and share. It is also what makes the franchise platform defensible — the algorithm, the data, and the benchmarking database are owned by Vitalis corporate.

### Medical Leadership

Dr. Truei brings an active concierge medicine practice and an established patient base in the Dallas market. The Medical Director arrangement provides clinical oversight, protocol development authority, and the physician credentialing required for certain services. This structure — a part-time Medical Director supported by full-time RNs and LVNs — is the standard operating model for high-performing longevity clinics and keeps the cost structure manageable while maintaining clinical integrity.

### Technology Infrastructure

Vitalis operates on a proprietary, HIPAA-compliant technology stack — not a licensed EMR platform. This is a deliberate architectural decision. Legacy EMR systems (Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth) are designed for acute care billing and are architecturally incompatible with the longitudinal optimization data model that powers the Vitalis Score. The proprietary stack includes:

- On-premise LLM inference server for clinical decision support and protocol recommendations
- Custom EMR with longevity-specific data models (biomarker time series, Vitalis Score history)
- HIPAA-compliant cloud backup with encrypted storage
- Lab API integrations via HL7 FHIR (Quest Diagnostics, LabCorp, and partner labs)
- Wearable data ingestion (Apple HealthKit, Oura, Whoop, Garmin)
- Franchise management dashboard for corporate oversight of all locations

This infrastructure is the subject of the AI architecture engagement and represents the single most important capital allocation decision in the business plan. It is what separates Vitalis from a well-decorated IV bar.

## Franchise Platform

### The Thesis

Dr. Barnes understands this model intuitively. Eye Express was not valuable because it had one location — it was valuable because the operating system, brand, and patient experience could be replicated. Vitalis is built with the same logic from day one. Every protocol, every technology decision, every staff training module is designed to be franchisable.

The difference between Vitalis and Eye Express is the AI backbone. The Vitalis Score algorithm improves with every member, at every location. A franchisee in Houston benefits from outcomes data generated in Dallas. The platform becomes more defensible with every location opened, every biomarker recorded, every optimization cycle completed. This is a network effect that a traditional franchise cannot replicate.

### Corporate Store Strategy

The first 2–3 locations are corporate-owned. This is not a capital constraint — it is a strategic requirement. The franchise disclosure document, the franchise operations manual, the training program, and the royalty structure must all be built on demonstrated, auditable unit economics. Selling franchises before proving the model is how franchise systems fail. Vitalis will not do that.
